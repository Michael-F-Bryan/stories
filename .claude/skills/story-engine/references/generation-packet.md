# Generation packet

One compact artefact: one packet for one active design thread; revise it in place as work changes rather than splitting it into competing forms. Fill this before planning a chapter, scene, arc beat, or revision target. Keep it current.

## Reader pull
What the reader should want next, fear next, or understand next.

## Promise movement
What promise is planted, tightened, complicated, or paid off; do not use a vague deferred state as a substitute.

## Character pressure
The character's immediate want, private fear, current misreading, social strategy, and cost of failure, kept compact and operational.

## Attention field
What the POV notices first, what it habitually ignores, and what it cannot interpret correctly yet; use this to drive character-owned detail selection.

## Causal spine
Treat the causal spine as the default planning and diagnostic lens:
First action → resistance → turn → choice → consequence → changed residue.
Not every beat has to dramatise each field separately; quieter units can record the changed understanding, relationship, or stakes as residue.

## Shape choice
Why this is a scene, chapter, beat sequence, or variation; where the turn lands; what structural shape best serves the pressure.

## Explanation budget
What must be explained now, what can wait, and what can stay implicit because the page can earn it later.

## Voice anchors
The stable voice facts that belong in the bible, plus one short ordinary spoken sample and one short ordinary internal sample to orient the voice.
Use the bible for enduring anchors.
Use the packet for fast-changing pressure or misreadings in the current state.
Avoid catchphrase templates and psych-label biographies; keep the anchor practical, behavioral, and speakable.

## Packet rule
If a detail does not affect pull, pressure, promise movement, or causal shape, leave it out of the packet.
If a detail is likely to change next session, keep it here instead of promoting it to canon.
