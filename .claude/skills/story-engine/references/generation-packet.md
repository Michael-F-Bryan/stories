# Generation packet

One compact artefact for one design pass. Fill this before planning a chapter, scene, arc beat, or revision target. Keep it current; do not split it into separate forms.

## Reader pull
What the reader should want next, fear next, or understand next.

## Promise movement
What promise is opening, tightening, paying off, or being deliberately deferred.

## Character pressure
The specific want, need, constraint, misread, threat, or obligation driving the character right now.

## Attention field
What belongs in focus, what can stay in the background, and what the reader must not be made to juggle yet.

## Causal spine
Pressure → action → changed situation → new pressure.
State the concrete action and the concrete change.

## Shape choice
Why this is a scene, chapter, beat sequence, or variation; where the turn lands; what structural shape best serves the pressure.

## Explanation budget
What must be explained now, what can wait, and what can stay implicit because the page can earn it later.

## Voice anchors
The stable voice facts that belong in the bible and the fast-changing wants or misreadings that belong in this packet/current state.
Use the bible for enduring anchors.
Use the packet for local pressure.
Avoid catchphrase mimicry and psych-label biographies; keep the anchor practical, behavioral, and speakable.

## Packet rule
If a detail does not affect pull, pressure, promise movement, or causal shape, leave it out of the packet.
If a detail is likely to change next session, keep it here instead of promoting it to canon.
