# Factions — evergreen canon

*Groups, their goals, their relationships. Current standings and season state live in ledgers.*

*Register warning: bible wit stays in the bible. Narration takes only the facts; the good lines belong to characters (candidates tagged inline).*

## The Gate Authority

The Republic's regulator. Charters licences, grades Gates, assigns terms-countdown response, runs assessments and verification terminals, buys essence at the standard buyback. Competent, underfunded outside Corvel, and institutionally allergic to admitting error (see: District Nine, "grading error", four years of tribunal hearings, nobody fired).

Wants: no Breaks, no headlines, budget renewal. Fears: term-drift statistics going mainstream; anything that makes the public ask what the System actually is.

## The Gate League

The commercial body: seasons, broadcasts, sponsorships, the Potential Index, assignment priority. The League turned survival infrastructure into the world's biggest sport, and it knows exactly which of those two things pays for the other.

Wants: narratives, ratings, sponsor renewals, no on-air fatalities. The League doesn't fix what the Index misses because the Index isn't broken *for its purpose* — its purpose is selling strikers to shampoo brands. (That last phrasing is a Theo line. He's not wrong.)

**Broadcast apparatus:** qualifier and fixture feeds run with shoulder-rig camera crews (a convention since Year 7) plus fixed aperture-side coverage. The Division D booth is **Ambrose Tan** (play-by-play; has called every League season since the first, fourteen of them; has seen everything twice) and **"Kef" Keffler** (colour; ex-striker; loud, generous, statistically illiterate in ways Pip finds physically painful).

## The majors (S-division)

Eight guild-backed franchises with feeder systems, analysts, and payrolls bigger than Halden's transit budget. The ones canon so far:

- **Vantage** — perennial number two, famous for discipline and depth. Older than the League itself: one of the scramble-era teams folded in at the Year 8 charter. Cass anchored their front line for nine years (Year 6–15) until Aldercourt. Runs an extensive feeder network, including Kestrel Nine.
- **Meridian Combine** — the number one. Corporate, polished, faintly sinister in the way of things with no visible weaknesses. (Develop on-page when relevant.)

## Kestrel Nine

Division D's top seed: a Vantage feeder affiliate run by captain **Dree Vann**, who develops talent efficiently and discards it punctually. As an affiliate, Kestrel Nine may decline promotion and pass the slot down the table (see mechanics: affiliates) — which it does, every season, legally and profitably, farming development minutes and standings bonuses one division below where its roster belongs. Poached Marc Osei from Grave Society eleven days before the registration deadline. The natural first rival: everything Grave Society isn't — funded, polished, and exactly where it wants to be.

## Grave Society

- Founded Year 15 by Cass Ferro, freshly out of Vantage and unsealing her savings: Aldercourt fell in the winter at Year 15's opening; she was licensed by the spring window and debuted that same season. Division D ever since.
- The name: the founding roster paid rent by taking **graveyard-term Gates** — apertures in their final term-week that no ranked team wanted (final-week bounty multipliers are the best risk-adjusted money in the division; see mechanics: escalation ladder). Somebody at the Authority desk called them "that grave society" and Cass filed the paperwork with it.
- One league win ever — *win* meaning a head-to-head fixture; qualifier clears pay the bills but don't count as wins: Year 15, their debut fixture, before the division adjusted to them. It's on the wall. The frame is dusted; nobody talks about it.
- **How they've survived seven licence reviews at the bottom of the table:** review weighs the whole file, and Grave Society's file is a freak — a 100% contract-completion rate and *zero casualty flags, ever*, across seven seasons of the Gates nobody else would take. Cass's one law is also a survival strategy. **This season the lifeline narrows:** the League's new broadcast-value weighting means clean-and-invisible no longer clears review on its own. Bottom eight this year likely means the licence.
- Current rank: 214 of 215. Broke, unsponsored (rent on the gym is 2,400/month and late more often than not), and — as of eleven days before deadline — five bodies for a six-slot roster.
- Home: the gym above **Marchetti's Laundry**, Corso Street, District Seven, Halden. Mrs Marchetti pretends not to notice the late rent because Cass walked her granddaughter home through the Year 18 siren night.
- Culture: Cass's one law — *nobody goes into a Gate unread* — plus whatever the five of them have instead of money: drills, dark humour, and Juno's cooking.

## The Halden Recovery Trust (and the insurers behind it)

The entity that administers District Nine's reconstruction levy. A payments office with the moral imagination of a spreadsheet. (Bea's phrasing. She's right.) The Break's insurance exclusion ("extradimensional incursion") was upheld in Year 14; the reconstruction bond runs thirty years; household shares follow the person, not the property. Background pressure on the Okafors, and Halden's civic grudge in miniature.

## Division D — the neighbours

Seventy-one teams; the ones canon so far, for fixture use:

- **Kestrel Nine** — top seed, Vantage affiliate (own section above).
- **Brickyard Union** — Halden's *other* D-team: dockworker co-op, six shareholders-slash-players, derby-match grudge with anyone from District Seven. Honest, slow, immovable.
- **The Palisades** — ex-military squad. Disciplined, dull, safe; the division's benchmark for "fine". Their captain files complaints the way other teams throw punches.
- **Sunder Bros.** — four actual brothers plus two men who regret their contracts. Brawlers; GateWatch darlings; casualty-flag machine.
- **Vesper Youth** — the Saint Vesper academy squad (third major, canon as of this line): teenagers whose boots cost more than Grave Society's van. Terrifying fundamentals, no adult supervision on comms.
- **Coldharbour FC** — fishing-town club, oldest licence in the division, perpetually 30th–40th. Their fans travel. Nobody knows why.
- **Redcap Circuit** — streamer-owned, chaotic, more subscribers than wins. Plays for content; occasionally, horrifyingly, the content is good.

## GateWatch

The fan forum. Fixture threads, technique frame-data, wipe compilations, meme culture (see: the Ganz Freeze). Collectively wrong about most things and occasionally, dangerously right — the term-drift statisticians post here. The story's ambient chorus: what GateWatch believes is what "everyone knows", which is rarely what's true.

Recurring handles (canon): **FrameData_Fen** — frame-data and telemetry poster, dry, rigorous, one of the term-drift statisticians; **ClearanceSale** — wipe-clip and meme channel; made the Ganz Freeze famous, would clip their own funeral.
