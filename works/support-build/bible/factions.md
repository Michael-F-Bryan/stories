# Factions — evergreen canon

*Groups, their goals, their relationships. Current standings and season state live in ledgers.*

## The Gate Authority

The Republic's regulator. Charters licences, grades Gates, assigns terms-countdown response, runs assessments and verification terminals, buys essence at the standard buyback. Competent, underfunded outside Corvel, and institutionally allergic to admitting error (see: District Nine, "grading error", four years of tribunal hearings, nobody fired).

Wants: no Breaks, no headlines, budget renewal. Fears: term-drift statistics going mainstream; anything that makes the public ask what the System actually is.

## The Gate League

The commercial body: seasons, broadcasts, sponsorships, the Potential Index, assignment priority. The League turned survival infrastructure into the world's biggest sport, and it knows exactly which of those two things pays for the other.

Wants: narratives, ratings, sponsor renewals, no on-air fatalities. The League doesn't fix what the Index misses because the Index isn't broken *for its purpose* — its purpose is selling strikers to shampoo brands.

## The majors (S-division)

Eight guild-backed franchises with feeder systems, analysts, and payrolls bigger than Halden's transit budget. The ones canon so far:

- **Vantage** — perennial number two, famous for discipline and depth. Cass anchored their front line for nine years until Aldercourt. Runs an extensive feeder network, including Kestrel Nine.
- **Meridian Combine** — the number one. Corporate, polished, faintly sinister in the way of things with no visible weaknesses. (Develop on-page when relevant.)

## Kestrel Nine

Division D's top seed and its most obvious escalator: a Vantage feeder affiliate run by captain **Dree Vann**, who develops talent efficiently and discards it punctually. Poached Marc Osei from Grave Society eleven days before registration. The natural first rival: everything Grave Society isn't — funded, polished, ascending — one division too low, on purpose, farming easy standings.

## Grave Society

- Founded Year 15 by Cass Ferro, freshly out of Vantage and unsealing her savings. Licensed into Division D and never out of it.
- The name: the founding roster paid rent by taking **graveyard-term Gates** — apertures in their final term-week that no ranked team wanted to touch. Somebody at the Authority desk called them "that grave society" and Cass filed the paperwork with it.
- One league win ever: Year 15, their debut fixture, before the division adjusted to them. It's on the wall. The frame is dusted; nobody talks about it.
- Current rank: 214 of 215. Broke, unsponsored, and — as of eleven days before deadline — five bodies for a six-slot roster.
- Home: the gym above **Marchetti's Laundry**, Corso Street, District Seven, Halden. Rent is late more often than not; Mrs Marchetti pretends not to notice because Cass walked her granddaughter home through the Year 18 siren night.
- Culture: Cass's one law — *nobody goes into a Gate unread* — plus whatever the five of them have instead of money: drills, dark humour, and Juno's cooking.

## The Halden Recovery Trust (and the insurers behind it)

The entity that administers District Nine's reconstruction levy. A payments office with the moral imagination of a spreadsheet. The Break's insurance exclusion ("extradimensional incursion") was upheld in Year 14; the levy runs thirty years; it follows the person, not the property. Background pressure on the Okafors, and Halden's civic grudge in miniature.

## GateWatch

The fan forum. Fixture threads, technique frame-data, wipe compilations, meme culture (see: the Ganz Freeze). Collectively wrong about most things and occasionally, dangerously right — the term-drift statisticians post here. The story's ambient chorus: what GateWatch believes is what "everyone knows", which is rarely what's true.
