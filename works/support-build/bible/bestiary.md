# Bestiary — evergreen canon

*Common gaunt types for D/C-grade interiors: enough nouns that fights are planned against known behaviour, never improvised. Folk names are GateWatch/hobbyist convention — the Authority uses catalogue numbers nobody says aloud. New types get added here when a chapter needs them, before drafting, not during.*

*Regard shorthand: what raises this gaunt's attention, and what its kill-pattern is. Fight clarity depends on these staying consistent.*

## Skitters (E–D)

Dog-sized, six-legged, chitin-plated rushers. Hunt in packs of five to twenty. Regard snaps to *motion* — a sprinting striker collects the whole pack; a still one gets ignored for the loudest teammate. Kill-pattern: swarm the legs, drag down, pile on. Individually trivial, collectively the most common cause of D-division casualty flags. Counter: hold a line, kill them at a choke, never run in the open.

## Bell jellies (D)

Translucent floating sacs, head-height to ceiling-height, drifting on nothing. Nearly blind; regard keys to *sound*, and they drift toward it in slow herds. On contact or kill they burst: a concussive ring that staggers everything in a room-sized radius (and rings every other jelly's regard). Counter: quiet teams walk around them; clever teams burst them *on purpose* on top of something else — the stagger doesn't discriminate.

## Palps (D–C)

Wall- and ceiling-clinging ambushers, flat as luggage until they drop. Regard keys to *noise directly beneath them*. Kill-pattern: drop, wrap, constrict — a palp on a player's shoulders is a two-person problem. Scouts exist substantially because palps do. Counter: eyes up, marks up, and never let the formation bunch under an overhang.

## Doorkeepers (C)

Bear-sized, slab-armoured, territorial. Squat at chokepoints — literal doorways, sluice gates, stair heads — and do not pursue beyond them. Regard keys to *proximity to the threshold they've claimed*. Kill-pattern: short, brutal charges that stop dead at their territory's edge. Counter: they must be tanked and killed at their own door, or the door found a way around; you cannot bait them out. Wardens measure themselves against doorkeepers.

## Weepers (C)

Human-silhouetted at a distance, which is the worst thing about them; up close the proportions are wrong and the face is a drain. Keen dark-sight; regard keys to *light and heat*, which they hate and approach. Kill-pattern: patient encirclement in the dark, then all at once. Counter: a caster's flare draws every weeper in a gallery — which is either a disaster or the whole plan.

## Rakes (C–B)

Long-limbed lopers, fast as a bad dream, flank-hunters. The unnerving one: regard appears to key to *assessed weakness* — rakes probe a formation and commit at whoever tests softest: the limper, the grey-out, the one who flinched. Kill-pattern: hit-and-recede until something's alone, then finish it. Counter: no soft targets — which for most teams is aspiration, and for a team with a working escort might be policy.

## Core guardians

Whatever a Gate's Core grew instead of an immune system: unique or near-unique per Gate, always the interior's apex, always keyed to defend the Core chamber. Survey reports name them per-Gate ("the crane-cab guardian"). Canon rule: a guardian's kit reflects its interior — a drowned warehouse grows a drowned thing.
