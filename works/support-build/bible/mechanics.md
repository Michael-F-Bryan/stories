# Mechanics — evergreen canon

*The power system. These rules are load-bearing: chapters must not contradict them, and schemes that exploit them must actually work when the reader checks.*

## The System

- Arrived with the Gates in Year 0. Nobody knows what it is. It has never spoken, announced, updated, or explained. It does exactly three observable things: awakens people into **classes**, displays **panels**, and ranks **skills** with use.
- **Panels** are a private HUD only the user perceives. Text can be shared by reading aloud, or projected publicly at Authority **verification terminals** (assessment centres, league check-ins). You cannot fake a projected panel; people have tried, expensively.
- **CORE RULE — the text is exact.** System text means precisely what it says, does everything it says, and does nothing it does not say. It does not use words loosely. Twenty-two years of evidence: every apparent exception on record turned out to be a misreading. The public knows this rule the way the public knows compound interest: in theory, and not in practice.
- The System never patches. There are no updates — only discoveries. Skill behaviour "found" in Year 22 worked identically in Year 2; nobody had read carefully enough.

## Awakening and classes

- ~1 in 800 people, ages 16–20, correlated with cumulative aperture proximity. The moment itself is quiet: a panel is simply there.
- The System assigns one **class** per person, permanently. ~140 classes are on the public registry. Frequency varies wildly: **Strikers** are nearly a third of all awakened; some classes have single-digit national counts.
- The Authority registry assigns each class a code on first recording (sequential within category). League culture sorts classes into six colloquial roles — striker, caster, warden, scout, mender, support — which is a broadcast convention, not a System category. The System has never named its own taxonomy.
- **Skills** are class-given, with exact text and a rank (I–X) that climbs with meaningful use. New skills appear at milestones the System does not announce in advance. Panels show skills only — no stats, no levels, no health bars. Bodies are still bodies: fitness, sleep, and training matter and are trained mundanely.
- **Techniques** are not skills. A technique is a human-developed application of a skill, named by players and broadcasters — [Ledger Cut], [Double Tap], [Curtain Call]. Skills are System-granted; techniques are craft and branding. Commentators name-drop techniques; the System has never acknowledged one.

## Focus

- Every skill activation draws on **Focus** — experienced as attention, not energy. Spending it feels like holding too many conversations at once.
- Focus is invisible (no bar, no number). Players learn their envelope by overdrawing it, which is why trainers exist.
- Overdraft symptoms in order: tunnel vision → flattened hearing → **grey-out** (skills refuse to fire) → in extremity, hours of clumsy, stupid exhaustion. Full recovery from a deep overdraft takes about a day; nothing known accelerates it except sleep and food.
- Sustained skills (auras, escorts, channelled effects) drain continuously; the drain scales with how much the effect is actually doing. An escort walking a flat corridor costs almost nothing; an escort through a rockslide bills accordingly.

## Parties

- A formal System structure, not a metaphor. Invite and acceptance are panel actions. Maximum eight slots; the League fields six.
- Party members perceive each other's names on HUD, a coarse condition light (steady / wavering / down), and can send **pings** — a directional attention-tap, like a tap on the shoulder from a distance.
- Many skills — most support-role skills — specify "party member" in their text and simply will not target outside the party. This is why support classes are close to untestable solo, and why so little is publicly known about the rare ones.

## Threat ("regard")

- Gaunts triage targets. The observed pattern is called **regard**: proximity, noise, damage dealt, and line of sight raise it; stillness and distance decay it. Wardens hold regard on purpose; everyone else manages it by instinct or dies educating their team.
- Regard is folk-science. The Authority publishes guidance; the forums publish better guidance; nobody has numbers, because the System doesn't share them.

## Gates

- A **Gate** is an aperture — a standing tear, door-sized to hangar-sized — opening into an interior space (**the far side**) that is larger than the aperture and obeys its own layout: tunnels, forests, flooded galleries, whatever. Interiors are stable per-Gate and mapped by scout survey.
- Every Gate contains **gaunts** (the common word for the fauna; taxonomies are hobbyist) and exactly one **Core** — a mass, crystalline or grown-into-the-terrain, that the Gate is somehow *for*. Destroy the Core and the Gate closes within minutes; everything inside it that came from the far side stops mattering.
- **Grading** (E, D, C, B, A, S) is an Authority density assessment from aperture instrumentation. It's good, not perfect. District Nine was a grading error.
- **Terms.** An uncleared Gate **Breaks** at the end of its term: the aperture ruptures wide and the far side empties into the street. Standard terms by grade: E ~90 days, D ~60, C ~40, B ~21, A ~10, **S ~72 hours**. An S-grade aperture is a national emergency by definition.
- Casualties inside a Gate are real casualties. There is no respawn, no safe-log-out, and the League's insurance actuaries are the grimmest people alive.
- **Loot:** essence (standard Authority buyback, paid per grade-adjusted unit), materials (chitin, alloys, botanicals — private markets), and rarely **artifacts**: objects with their own System text. Artifact text follows the CORE RULE like everything else.

## The Gate League

- Charters teams, runs the season, owns the broadcast, and — critically — controls **assignment priority**: higher-ranked teams get first refusal on richer Gates. Sponsorship tiers key off division. Promotion is worth more than prize money.
- **Structure, Year 22:** 215 licensed teams. Eight **majors** (S-division) above four divisions: A (24), B (48), C (64), D (71). Roster: six fielded, eight registered max. **A team that cannot field six forfeits its season.**
- **Season shape:** spring registration → **qualifiers** (each team clears assigned Gates on broadcast; scored on clear time, damage economy, control penalties, casualty flags) → split fixtures: head-to-head **twin-Gate races** (two instanced apertures of the same interior; first Core kill wins) → autumn playoffs and relegation.
- **Relegation:** bottom eight of Division D at season's end face **licence review** — in practice, demotion to freelance E-grade work, which pays like it sounds.
- Scoring rewards *clean*: a slow no-injury clear can outscore a fast sloppy one. Casualty flags are score poison. This is canon and will matter tactically.

## The Potential Index

- The League's headline metric for individual players, computed from output telemetry: damage coefficients, clear contributions, growth curve. The number that gets you recruited, sponsored, and shipped in fantasy leagues.
- **It is a League product, not a System product.** The System has never ranked a person.
- Support effects are largely invisible to output telemetry. Classes whose skills don't move the damage ledger assess as **UNINDEXED** — an official grade that reads, socially, as *zero*. Ren's assessment answer is technically correct and nobody thinks about it: unindexed means *unmeasured*, not *nothing*.

## The Attendant class

- Registry code **SVC-001** — the first service-category class ever recorded, Year 0. (Public trivia; registry nerds notice, nobody wonders.)
- Eleven recorded nationally in twenty-two years. Currently three: two employed as majors' valets — a status accessory, carrying the ace's bags on camera — and Ren.
- Public knowledge about the class is close to nil, because support skills can't be tested solo and no Attendant has ever played league.
- **Ren's starting panel (canon text, exact):**
  - **[ESCORT] I** — *Designate a party member as your charge. While you escort your charge, they move 15% faster and cannot stumble.*
  - **[CARRY] I** — *Take up a willing party member's burden. What you carry costs them nothing.*
  - **[ATTEND] I** — *Wait upon your charge. While you attend, you perceive what your charge perceives.*
- Observed costs and limits get recorded in `ledgers/` as chapters establish them — the bible holds only the text, which does not change.

## Rules for writing mechanics on the page

- Every exploit must survive a careful reader: quote the text, show the reading, show the cost.
- Skills have prices — Focus, positioning, opportunity cost. A fight won by skill-text cleverness should still cost something.
- Lore arrives through pressure (a fight, an argument, a price), never as a systems lecture. Pip can recite numbers *because that's characterisation*.
