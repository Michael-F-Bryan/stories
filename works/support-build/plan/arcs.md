# Support Build — arc outline (SPOILERS — owner does not read)

*Arc-level plan. Chapter counts are estimates, not contracts; the loop adjusts. Twists and reveals are designed here and tracked in `ledgers/promises.md` once planted on the page.*

## Craft directives (owner guidance through ch. 3 — binding on every arc)

1. **No beat may depend on a quotable line.** Payoffs are events, not sentences (plain-speech rule). Anywhere this plan imagines a "cool line" landing (technique branding, broadcast moments, reveal punchlines), the *situation* carries it; the prose stays ordinary. When the buried-layer truths land (Aldercourt, the evacuation reading), they land as plainly stated facts — a document read aloud, a survivor saying a normal sentence — never as implication the reader must decode.
2. **Introduction schedule (first-mention discipline at arc scale).** Nothing pays off before it has been introduced and developed on a published page. Current debts and deadlines:
   - **Regard/gaunt behaviour: never yet on-page.** Ch. 4 must introduce gaunt types and regard mechanics through the team using them (Pip briefing in the van / first skitter contact) BEFORE any tactic exploits them.
   - **SVC-001**: visible on the ch. 1 panel but never discussed. Needs a proper on-page beat (registry-trivia conversation, Pip is the natural mouth) before arc 2's registry digging can matter.
   - **Term-drift**: introduce as GateWatch forum noise (FrameData_Fen) in arc 2, well before arc 3 evidence.
   - **Aldercourt**: the word must enter the story casually (broadcast reference, "worst wipe in league history" texture) at least several chapters before anyone connects it to Cass.
   - **Kestrel Nine / Marc**: Marc has been a phone call; he needs an on-page scene before the rematch can carry weight.
   - **The Response Wing, buyback economics, twin-Gate format**: each needs a lived introduction before the plot leans on it.
3. **Gear is a progression axis now (owner request: the story was "remarkably silent" on equipment).** Ch. 3's loadout scene opens the thread: gear improves visibly as money arrives (first buyback → first upgrades — this is the classic progression pleasure and it was missing from the plan). Planned gear payoffs: Juno's banked-coal pots earn their wrapping at the Stacks; **Cass's hammer is the Core-breaker** (Cores die to blunt force; wardens carry the hammer — introduce plainly at the Stacks' Core chamber, which also quietly explains her kit's history); Ren's kit evolves chapter over chapter as the team learns what a support actually needs (rope → harness rigs → eventually the evacuation kit assembling in plain sight, which rhymes with the buried layer).
4. **Wonder beats are scheduled, not incidental:** the first aperture crossing (ch. 4), the first sight of a far-side interior's wrongness, the Core chamber. Each gets unhurried, plainly-seen room. No quips inside a wonder beat, no trailer narration.
5. **Motif budgets apply to the plan too.** The exact-text theme stays load-bearing but must not decorate every chapter; the read-twice family, the butler gag, and the money-for-Focus vocabulary are rationed per the skill's budgets. Emotional beats are played straight (earnestness rule) — the plan's sincere moments (family, team-becoming-family) need no ironic cover.

## Arc 1 — "Sixth" (ch. 1–9, approx.)

**Question:** Can the worst team in the league survive its own qualifier?
**Ren's axis:** From unwanted to load-bearing: Escort and Carry proven in training, then under fire.

- Ch. 1: Assessment humiliation → the ad → tryout → signed with minutes to spare. Qualifier posted: the Stacks, C-grade, nine days. (Plants: exact-text motif, "cannot stumble", Carry's "burden" wording, unindexed = unmeasured, Cass reads text like a lawyer, Marc's defection, levy debt, SVC-001 trivia deferred.)
- Ch. 2–3: Training week. Escort's mechanics established honestly (Focus drain scaling, sight-line requirement, single-charge limit). Team dynamics: Theo's camera problem named; Juno's overcasting named; first Carry experiment — Ren takes Juno's *spell upkeep* as a burden and greys out in four minutes: burden includes non-physical loads, and the price is real. Bea/levy runner: first stipend payment. Kestrel Nine media event sets rivalry temperature.
- **The Stacks — qualifier Gate spec (fixed now, drafted against later):** C-grade, aperture in the old container port; interior = drowned warehouse rows — racking canyons prone to collapse, black water waist-to-chest deep across the low thirds. Fauna: **sodden** (waterlogged grapplers, ambush from beneath the surface — regard keys to ripples/vibration; add to bestiary when first on-page), palps in the high racking, a doorkeeper at the sluice-gate choke, skitter packs on the dry mezzanines. Core in a crane cab above the flooded floor; guardian: a drowned thing (design at ch. 5, per bestiary's guardian rule). Term remaining at assignment: 26 days; GS scheduled at day 9. Survey data last updated Year 20 — **the flood level has shifted since; Pip's map is wrong in the low thirds** (the set-piece complication, established fairly via the survey date on-page — and ch. 3 published the two-map junction protocol, so the failure is doubly fair: their plan distrusted the survey; reality is worse than their worst case). Four teams have wiped here since Year 19; median time-to-wipe eleven minutes (Pip's stat, ch. 1).
- **Ch. 4 additional duties (per craft directives):** the aperture crossing is the serial's first wonder beat — unhurried, plainly seen. Gaunt types and regard mechanics get their on-page introduction through use (van briefing + first contact) before any tactic exploits them. Attend's 30-second door test happens as planned on-page (pays ch. 1's deferral; establish its felt cost). The broadcast layer is present from entry (shoulder-rig, Theo's shielded position from ch. 3's diagram). Cass's hammer is introduced as the Core-breaker plainly when the Core plan is discussed.
- Ch. 4–6: **The Stacks qualifier** (2.5-chapter set piece per the spec above). Structured as: clean opening third (the plan works) → complication (mis-mapped flood level; Pip's survey invalidated mid-run) → the support-build answer: Escort's "cannot stumble" through collapsing racking, Attend giving Pip's eyes to the whole party through blackwater, Carry taking Doc's triage burden in the extraction. Score lands mid-table on damage economy + zero casualty flags: **the League's own clean-clear scoring becomes their meta**. Mini-cliff: broadcast clips Ren's telemetry as a rounding error — GateWatch notices a team that cleared with a "passenger".
- Ch. 7: Aftermath/payment chapter. Money on the page (buyback, levy quarter paid). The "passenger" narrative hurts; Theo defends Ren on camera and freezes; cost honest. Cass, privately, asks Ren to read her his full panel — and stops at Attend's text for one beat too long. (First Aldercourt tell, unexplained.)
- Ch. 8–9: Second qualifier vs schedule congestion — a graveyard-term D-Gate taken for money, the team's old speciality, now with a sixth. First deliberate technique-naming on broadcast (the team brands Ren's plays to fight the passenger narrative — played as in-story commercial necessity, argued about like a business decision; per craft directive 1, the branding beat must NOT hinge on the coined name being a cool line). First gear upgrades from the Stacks buyback land here (craft directive 3 — visible, itemized through use, not shopping-list prose). Arc climax: qualifying placement secured — not relegation-safe, but alive, seeded into the split. Kestrel Nine fixture confirmed as the split opener; Marc gets his first on-page scene here (introduction debt, directive 2). Arc ends on the draw, not a cliff.

## Arc 2 — "Meta" (ch. 10–22, approx.)

**Question:** What happens when the league starts taking the support build seriously?
**Ren's axis:** Rank-ups, first milestone skill, and the discovery that being studied is a tax.

- Split fixtures: twin-Gate races force direct comparison; Grave Society's clean-clear scoring vs faster teams. Opponents begin countering: forced-stumble terrain tactics (testing "cannot stumble" edges — text holds, Focus bill explodes), party-slot denial, targeting the Attendant first ("kill the healer" logic reborn — regard-manipulation counterplay).
- Kestrel Nine opener: loss, and an honest one — Marc knows the old roster's habits cold. Rematch seeded for arc climax. Dree Vann buys Ren a coffee and offers him a two-way: villain move played as pure market logic.
- Ren milestone skill #1: **[USHER] I** — *Open the way for your party. Doors know you.* (Deliberately service-diction, deliberately weird; mechanical floor: barred/blocked/jammed openings yield to him inside Gates. Ambiguity to be explored honestly; SVC-001 diction pattern now visible to sharp readers.)
- Juno's arc pays first: Carry can hold her overcast *upkeep*, but the fix that matters is the why (a scene, not a speech — her old team's wipe she outlasted by being empty).
- Theo camera arc: the Freeze breaks not by courage speech but by role redesign — Attend lets him fight blind to the cameras through Pip's eyes for one crucial pull; partial, priced (nausea, Focus).
- Mid-arc reveal (human scale): Ren, digging class registry history for counterplay, finds the sealed Aldercourt roster and Cass's notation — confronts her with a document, because that's who he is. **Cass tells the truth: there were survivors.** The reader gets Aldercourt in full; the cosmology stays buried. Trust breaks and reforms with stakes: she's been using the team's licence as a key she never turns.
- Arc climax: Kestrel Nine rematch, won on a scheme the reader can check (their counter-support tactics have a Focus-economy hole; Pip's numbers find it; Ren's Usher line-breaks the twin-Gate's shared choke). Promotion to Division C mathematically live. Cliff: the Authority quietly re-grades a Halden port Gate upward — one line in a fixture email, wrong-feeling.

## Arc 3 — "Term" (ch. 23–34, approx.)

**Question:** What is the schedule actually for?
**Ren's axis:** From player to practitioner: the class's purpose surfaces.

- Division C: better teams, broadcast pressure, first sponsor (comic and costly). Term-drift plotline moves from forum noise to on-page evidence via Pip; Authority stonewalls; the team starts reading Gates the way Ren reads contracts.
- Milestone skill #2: **[HOUSEHOLD] I** — *Those under your roof are your charges.* (Party-scale escort; "roof" definable by held/enclosed space — the evacuation kit assembling in plain sight.)
- Aldercourt thread: with C-division assignment priority and Cass's testimony, the team forces a sanctioned survey re-entry (legal-thriller-by-way-of-dungeon-crawl chapters — Ren's fine-print instincts vs Authority doctrine). **They bring people out.** Not all; twenty-two years is long; the recovered are time-thinned and strange. Public detonation: the record opens, the League's Year 15 feed-cut surfaces, Cass's notation burns off.
- The reveal reframes rather than resolves: survivors describe the far side's deep layers — something large, slow, and patient moving; gaunt migrations running *ahead* of it. First on-page cosmology evidence. Term-drift and the survivor testimony now rhyme; nobody official will say the word "evacuation" yet.
- Arc climax: the re-graded port Gate misses its revised term projection — Break risk goes public mid-playoff-window. Cliff into the final arc: sirens as fixture email arrives — the League postpones nothing.

## Arc 4 — "Break" (ch. 35–44, approx.)

**Question:** What is a support build for?
**Climax:** The Halden port Break, per `pitch.md`: undismissible, unclearable, survivable only by moving people *through*. The team's whole toolkit — Escort/Usher/Household chains, Attend relays for sight in the surge, Carry rotations for the ones who can't walk, Cass holding doors, Juno finally spending everything correctly, Theo fighting cameraless in front of every camera in the Republic, Pip routing, Doc's triage carried — executed as the longest set piece in the serial, scored zero by the Index in real time.

- Milestone skill #3 unlocks mid-Break from scale of service, not combat: **[STEWARD]** (text designed when reached; must be earned by the eleven hundred, not by plot need).
- Aftermath chapters (2–3): costs kept on-page — casualties among secondary cast are permitted and should be chosen for weight, not shock; the levy paid off in the least triumphant way possible (Break compensation, bitter symmetry with District Nine); the League absorbing the lesson commercially (support draft class, new Index column, Ren's class code on it).
- Final chapter: the gym, the second photograph, the noodle argument. Small, warm, earned.

## Standing cautions for the chapter loop

- The sports story must work for a reader who never gets the cosmology. Every arc's fixture stakes are real stakes.
- No prophecy voice, no portent. Buried-layer evidence is always a document, a statistic, a survivor's plain sentence — never atmosphere.
- Escalate prices with ranks: every new Escort/Carry/Attend application costs visibly more Focus; grey-out consequences stay real.
- Kestrel Nine stays human; Dree Vann is market logic, not malice. The Authority is tired, not evil. The League is a league.
