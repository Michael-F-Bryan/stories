## Cross-skill rulings

- **"Charge" is per-skill.** Escort's charge, Attend's charge, and (future) Household's charges are separate designations that may point at different people. Cass catches this in training (ch. 2–3 scene candidate — she asks the question like a lawyer, because she is one about this).
- **Designation is a panel action** — deliberate, takes about a breath, can't be done unconscious. Re-designating has a short lag at Rank I that shrinks with rank.
- **Rank ladder intent (design, adjust as earned):** Rank I = one charge/one burden; capacity and re-designation speed grow with rank; second simultaneous charge around IV; the class's ceiling is capacity, not intensity — it scales toward *many people*, because of what the class is for (see pitch.md).
- **Panels never transmit.** Attend shares senses; panels are System-to-user, not sensory (bible rule). Ruled day one, established early in training so no reader festers on it.

## [ESCORT] — *Designate a party member as your charge. While you escort your charge, they move 15% faster and cannot stumble.*

- **"While you escort"** = while Ren is actively guiding the charge's movement: he must perceive them and be attending to their route. It is a job, not a buff — his attention is the fuel line. If he stops paying attention, the escort lapses (System is literal about the verb).
- **"Cannot stumble" covers:** slips, trips, missteps, tangled feet, footing loss on collapse/ice/wet steel, knockdown *via footwork failure*, ladder and rope falls where footing is the failure point.
- **Does NOT cover:** being bodily thrown or lifted (not a stumble), unconsciousness, deliberate dives, falls where there was never footing to lose.
- **Boundary case [designed discovery, arc 2]:** forced knockback converts to a controlled slide — the feet never tangle, momentum still has to go somewhere, and the Focus bill spikes hard. Discovered when an opponent tries stumble-forcing terrain as counterplay.
- **15% is movement speed only** — not attack speed, not cast speed. Stacks multiplicatively with the charge's own movement skills (Theo + Escort is a stupid number; the cost scales to match).
- **Cost:** continuous drain scaling with terrain adversity and charge speed. Flat corridor ≈ nothing; collapsing racking at a sprint ≈ minutes of Ren's whole envelope.
- **Sight-line through Attend [designed discovery, arc 2]:** "perceiving your charge" is satisfied through *borrowed* senses while attending a third party — the escort-relay combo. Double drain plus nausea; the class's kit composes with itself, which is the real reveal.

## [CARRY] — *Take up a willing party member's burden. What you carry costs them nothing.*

- **"Costs them nothing" is exact:** zero, not reduced. The price does not vanish — it moves to Ren, converted into his coin: mass costs his muscles plus a Focus tax; a carried skill-upkeep costs his Focus at the *owner's* burn rate, unmitigated by their efficiency. He is always the least efficient place for the load — that's the balancing law. Grey-out is the ceiling on every clever use.
- **"Burden" ladder (the definition ladder IS the progression):**
  1. Physical loads — packs, gear, a stretcher (ch. 2, trivial case, establishes the skill works).
  2. **Skill upkeep** — a sustained channel's drain (ch. 3 experiment: Ren takes Juno's [KINDLE] feed and greys out in about four minutes; proves the category and its price).
  3. Aggregated small loads — the team's collective kit weight in an extraction (Stacks, ch. 6).
  4. [designed discovery, arc 2–3] Non-physical durable loads: a debuff's ongoing effect? Ruled YES for *sustained* effects (a toxin's spread is an ongoing cost), NO for instantaneous states (an injury is not a burden; a wound already happened). This line — cost vs. state — is the skill's legal spine and gets argued on-page.
  5. [arc 4 candidate, keep only if checkable+priced per arcs.md] A dying man's final skill activation carried to completion. Decision gate at arc 3.
- **"Willing"** = explicit consent per burden, revocable; the unconscious cannot consent (hard limit; drama engine, not loophole).
- **One burden at a time at Rank I.**

## [ATTEND] — *Wait upon your charge. While you attend, you perceive what your charge perceives.*

- **Shares senses:** sight and hearing at Rank I; touch/proprioception join at higher ranks (design intent ~Rank IV; earn it).
- **Simultaneity cost:** Ren's own senses dim while attending — he is a worse witness of his own surroundings, motion-sick within minutes at Rank I. Attending while moving is a trained skill (training-arc material); attending while *fighting* is arc-2-Theo-plot material, from the charge's side.
- **The charge feels nothing** — no ping, no presence. (Ruled deliberately: the privacy implications are a live ethical thread the team itself raises in training. Cass's line of questioning. Consent norms get set inside the team on-page — canon once published.)
- **Range:** party-standard (same as pings); breaks on party exit.
- **No panels, ever** (see cross-skill).
