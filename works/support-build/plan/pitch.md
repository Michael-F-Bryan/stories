# Support Build — expanded pitch (SPOILERS — owner does not read)

*This file holds the full premise including everything the reader doesn't know yet. Never quote, summarize, or allude to specifics in chat, commits, or public-facing files.*

## The premise under the premise

The surface serial is a sports-underdog progression story: unindexed support rookie joins the worst team in the league, the meta is wrong, they climb. All of that is real and must work on its own terms — the league arcs are not a decoy, they're the vehicle.

The buried layer: **the System is an evacuation protocol.** The Gates are not an invasion; they are infrastructure being connected, and the gaunts are fauna displaced ahead of something slower and larger moving through the far side's deep layers. Classes are jobs in a civil-defence plan written by something that thinks in logistics: Strikers clear routes, Bulwarks hold doors, Menders run triage, Trackers survey, and Attendants — registry code SVC-001, the *first* service class ever recorded — exist to move people who can't fight through hostile territory: escort them so they cannot stumble, carry what would kill them to carry, attend them so no one is lost sight of. The class nobody wanted is a lifeboat officer's commission. Humanity built a spectator sport on top of a fire drill and ranked everyone by how loudly they can hit things.

Term-drift (Gates' terms shortening year over year, currently forum-statistician fringe knowledge) is the schedule tightening.

## Aldercourt (Cass's secret)

Year 15, Vantage's front line took a B-grade Gate at Aldercourt on broadcast. The wipe that made the city a byword was not a wipe: deep in the Gate the team found **people** — survivors of a Break from three years earlier, alive on the far side, time-thinned but human. The broadcast feed was cut on League orders ("casualty protocol"). In the scramble to extract survivors instead of the Core, four of Vantage's six died; the Gate was resealed by Authority emergency doctrine with survivors still inside. The official record: a wipe caused by captain error. Cass refused to sign the clean version; the compromise that kept her out of prison and the survivors out of the record was a sealed conduct notation and her walking away from everything. She founded Grave Society to keep a licence, because a licence means Gate access, and she has spent seven years quietly failing to find a legal way back to a deep layer. She has stopped believing she ever will. Then she signs a kid whose class is literally built for extraction.

Cass does not know the evacuation-protocol truth. She knows one fact the world doesn't: people can survive inside Gates. The story's engine is these two half-truths finding each other.

## Intended ending (design now, earn later)

Final arc: Halden's own overdue reckoning — a mis-graded A-Gate in the port district hits term during the playoff window; a city-scale Break that cannot be cleared, only survived. Standard doctrine (evacuate the district, write off the trapped) collides with what the team now knows: the Gate interior itself is survivable *if civilians can be moved through it* to the far side's stable shallows until the surge passes. The climax inverts the entire series' value system on camera: a live broadcast where the Index telemetry reads zero — no damage dealt for twenty straight minutes of the most consequential footage in League history — while an Attendant with a full party escort-chains eleven hundred civilians through the far side. Ren's final milestone skill unlocks not from combat but from scale of service. Aldercourt's record is forced open in the aftermath; the survivors' recovery (some, not all — twenty-two years is long) is the denouement's bitter-sweetness. The League survives by absorbing the lesson the way leagues do: the season after, there's a support draft class, and the Index has a new column with Ren's class code on it.

The last image belongs to the team, not the world: Grave Society's gym, the dusty Year 15 frame joined by exactly one new photograph, and the six of them arguing about noodles.

## Why this premise fits the constraints

- Chapter engine (league fixtures) is genuinely separable from the mystery — early chapters are pure sports-progression and must satisfy as such.
- The buried layer pays the "exact text" motif off at maximum scale: the class descriptions were job descriptions all along. Careful readers can get there early from planted evidence (SVC-001, "charge/burden/wait upon" service diction, valet culture as misuse); casual readers get a twist that plays fair.
- Cass's secret gives the mid-game a human-scale reveal before the cosmology-scale one, keeping escalation stepped.
- The ending's set-piece is the thesis in action instead of a speech: nobody ever has to say "support classes mattered all along" out loud.
