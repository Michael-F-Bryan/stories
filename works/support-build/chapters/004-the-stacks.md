index: 4
title: The Stacks
description: "Grave Society enters the flooded Stacks on a live broadcast, where rusted aisles, black water, and another team make the clearance dangerous."
---

"The broadcast goes live in ninety seconds," the producer said.

Ren stood beside Grave Society's van at the old container port and checked the straps on Doc's pack. Beyond the temporary League barriers, a crowd had gathered between two empty loading bays. Most of them were holding phones above their heads. The League had mounted fixed cameras around the concrete apron, and the shoulder-rig operator was already filming Cass as she signed the entry sheet.

The Gate stood thirty metres away.

The aperture looked as thin as a cut in the air until Ren stood in front of it. Then it showed part of a warehouse that could not fit anywhere behind it, with rusted racking rising over black water beneath a high corrugated roof. The port's morning wind moved across Ren's face. Nothing inside the Gate moved with it.

Pip had given him the last briefing in the van. Gaunts were the animals that lived inside Gates. They chose targets through a pattern players called regard. Proximity and noise mattered. Moving or attacking mattered too, although each type reacted differently.

"The first dry section usually has skitters," she had said. "They're six-legged pack hunters, about the size of dogs. They follow movement, so when Cass draws their regard, everyone else stays still until Theo starts the kill. The flooded aisles have sodden. They lie under the water and feel the ripples through the floor. If we have to cross water, only the person moving should move."

"And if one catches someone?" Ren had asked.

"It pulls them under and holds them there."

She had said it in the same voice she used for corridor times. Ren had checked the release on his stretcher harness twice after that.

Cass returned the League's tablet and gathered the team behind the painted entry line. Her shield covered most of her body from Ren's angle. The hammer from the gym hung at her right hip.

"That hammer is for the Core," she said when she saw him looking. "Cores break more reliably under blunt force than they do under a blade. If we reach the crane cab, I do the breaking."

The producer held up five fingers to the camera operator and began counting down. "Ilyas, left side." He took his place beside Cass. She had put Theo on the far right, with her shield and Juno between him and the lens. Theo looked at the aperture. He had said nothing since they turned into the port gates.

Cass faced the team. "We use the plan we trained. Pip reads the ground. Nobody steps where she hasn't cleared. Ren uses Carry for Doc's pack and keeps Escort and Attend off until the door test. We leave if the wet map is wrong."

The producer dropped her last finger. A red light came on above the shoulder camera, and Cass stepped through the aperture.

---

Cass's shield vanished across the edge of the aperture, followed by the rest of her. Pip and Juno crossed after her. Ren reached for Carry while Doc was still beside him. The medical pack settled across his shoulders, and he followed Juno through.

One boot came down on cold steel, and the port disappeared.

Ren stopped because Cass had ordered them to stop, but for several seconds he would not have been able to walk anyway. The warehouse stretched farther than the entire container yard outside. Racking towers ran away in parallel rows until yellow haze swallowed them. Each tower rose ten storeys towards a roof held up by steel trusses thick enough to carry trains. Some of the racks still held containers with company logos on them, though several logos belonged to companies Ren had never heard of. Others held long blocks of dark material strapped to pallets. Water covered the floor beyond the receiving platform and reflected the lights without a ripple.

Behind him, the aperture showed the port crowd through a door-shaped opening. People outside were cheering. Their voices became faint as soon as they reached the warehouse.

The air smelled of rust and stagnant water. Morning light came through the aperture and made a door-shaped patch on the steel floor. Above it, the warehouse roof continued for another ten storeys. Somewhere beyond the first racking row, metal knocked once against metal.

Theo came through with his swords drawn. Doc followed him, and the shoulder-rig operator crossed last. The live timer on the side of the camera read 00:18.

"We'll do the door test here," Cass said.

Pip stood at the edge of the receiving platform and looked into the first aisle. Ren released Carry, and Doc's pack returned to him. Then Ren designated Pip as his charge and reached for Attend.

Ren stood behind Pip and activated Attend.

His own view faded. The colour drained from Cass's shield and the platform under his boots. At the same time, he could see from Pip's lower height. She was looking up into the racking, following every cross-brace and shadowed pallet. He heard Cass breathing to his left through his own ears, and through Pip's ears he heard water moving somewhere ahead of them.

Pip looked over her shoulder. Ren saw his own face from three metres away while he was still looking at the back of her head. His stomach turned hard enough that he put one hand on his knee.

"Keep looking forward," he said.

"Sorry. I forgot."

Cass watched the timer. At thirty seconds she said, "That's thirty. Stop there."

Ren released the skill. His normal sight returned at once, but the warehouse tilted when he stood upright. Juno caught his elbow and waited until he nodded.

"Tell me what happened," Cass said.

"I got her sight and hearing. Mine were still there, but they became dull. When she turned around, I saw myself and nearly threw up." Ren took the water bottle Doc offered him and drank once. "The Focus cost was low while she stood still. I could use it again today, but I shouldn't use it while either of us is moving until we've trained with it."

"I agree." Cass looked to Pip. "Anything from your side?"

"No. I couldn't tell he was there."

The camera operator shifted closer to catch her answer. Theo kept his eyes on the first aisle.

Cass waited until Ren had taken Doc's pack again. The live timer passed 01:12, and Grave Society moved away from the door.

---

Pip raised one fist at the mouth of the third aisle before Ren saw what had stopped her. Six red marks appeared in his sight as her skill picked out shapes behind a stack of plastic drums. A skitter showed part of its head around the drums: a low wedge of black shell, with two forelegs hooked over the floor.

Cass moved into the aisle alone and struck the face of her shield with the head of her hammer.

The hammer strike echoed along the racking. Six skitters came out at once. They were longer than Ren had expected, low to the ground, their shells fitted together in overlapping plates. Their feet made a rapid dry tapping on the steel floor.

Cass used [DRAW REGARD]. Her warden skill demanded the attention of every enemy near her, and all six heads turned towards the shield. The pack rushed her together.

She planted her feet and used [HOLD FAST]. The skill fixed her in place while her body still took the impact.

The first skitter hit the shield and bounced sideways. The others piled into it and scraped their claws against the composite. Cass stayed exactly where she was. Juno held still. Pip held still. Ren could feel Theo beside him waiting for the angle they had trained.

Cass shoved the pack back half a metre. "Theo, take them."

Theo moved before the skitters recovered.

His first step crossed the open floor too quickly for Ren's eyes to follow. One sword cut through the joint behind a skitter's head. The landed cut fed his next one, and he went through the pack without breaking the chain. The shoulder camera swung towards him from the other side of Cass's shield. Ren saw Theo notice the lens. His next step shortened.

Then Cass leaned into the pack, and her shield blocked the camera's view of him. Theo looked at the skitter in front of him and finished the cut. The chain kept going.

It was over in twelve seconds.

The last skitter folded against the base of the racking. Theo stepped away from it, wiped one blade on a rag he pulled from his belt, and checked the aisle ahead.

"Six contacts down at three minutes and forty-two seconds," the operator said for the feed. "Grave Society has entered clean."

Pip crouched beside the nearest body and touched two fingers to the wet shell. "They came through the water recently. Less than an hour."

"The survey has them nesting on the mezzanine," Juno said.

"They still might be. These six weren't."

Cass looked towards the dark aisle beyond the bodies. "Keep moving."

They reached the first ladder at 04:31 and climbed one level to avoid a shallow flooded bay. Pip went first and marked the underside of each platform before Cass committed the rest of them. Two palps lay flat against the steel above the fourth landing. Their pale bodies spread across the beams so evenly that Ren had mistaken them for insulation.

Pip's red marks made the shapes obvious. She put an arrow through the first before it could drop. The second peeled itself from the beam and fell towards Cass.

Juno opened one of her wax-wrapped pots. The cloth beneath the first layer was dry. She tipped a black coal into her palm, fed it until flame covered her fingers, and sent a narrow sheet of fire across the falling palp. It struck the platform already curling in on itself.

Juno closed her hand, put the fire out, and rewrapped the pot.

At the top of the ladder, the shoulder-rig operator had to turn sideways to fit between the rail and Cass's shield. Theo waited on the far landing with his back to the lens. When they moved again, his place in the formation stayed hidden behind Juno.

For the next three aisles, the formation worked as it had in the gym. Pip cleared the route while Cass controlled the front, and Theo killed anything that reached her. Juno only used fire when the racking made a sword unsafe. Ren carried Doc's pack and watched the three bad crossings ahead of them on Pip's maps. After the door test, Carry was the only skill he kept running.

At 07:06, they reached the stairs down to the first junction.

---

The water mark was supposed to be on the second pillar.

Pip stood on the lowest dry stair and aimed her torch across the junction. The beam found the first pillar, then climbed. A faded white survey stripe showed through the grime about a metre above the floor.

Black water covered it.

Pip moved the light higher. The water reached the third cross-brace, more than a metre above the mark on her wet map. It filled both ground-level aisles and ran under the stairs behind the next racking row. The dry route they had planned to take towards the sluice gate no longer existed.

Cass checked the route behind them. The timer on Ilyas's camera read 07:18. "We're leaving. Same formation. Pip, take us back."

Ilyas walked backwards down one stair to keep Cass and the drowned survey stripe in the same shot. His heel came down past the edge of the dry tread and entered the water.

Pip's head snapped towards him. "Don't move."

Ilyas froze with one foot submerged. A circle spread away from his shin and crossed the junction.

The surface settled, and then a second circle appeared beside the first. It moved towards him from under the racking.

"Sodden," Pip said.

Cass turned and drove the bottom of her shield into the water between Ilyas and the oncoming ripple. The impact drew a wave across the junction. Three more trails changed direction under the surface and came towards her.

Ilyas pulled his foot back. A pale hand closed around his ankle.

He hit the stairs on his side and grabbed the rail with both hands. Cass caught the shoulder strap of his armour. For a second they held him between them, Cass above the water and the sodden below it. Another pale arm came over the stair edge and locked around his knee. It pulled hard enough to tear one hand off the rail.

The camera struck the step and kept broadcasting as Ilyas went under.
