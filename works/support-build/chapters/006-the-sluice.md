description: "A doorkeeper guards the only solid stair, leaving Grave Society a narrow bridge, an injured rival, and no safe route back."

The bridge was wide enough for Cass and her shield. It was not wide enough for anyone to pass her.

Ren stayed at the mouth with the others. The steel deck crossed six metres of open water to the concrete tower. A handrail ran down each side, orange with rust. The doorkeeper waited in front of the tower door with its forelimbs spread across the threshold.

Cass took three steps onto the bridge.

The doorkeeper charged.

Its first stride cracked the concrete at the doorway. The next two brought it across the bridge faster than something that size should have moved. Cass set the bottom of her shield against the deck and used [HOLD FAST].

The doorkeeper hit her with its shoulder.

Ren felt the bridge jump under his boots. Cass stayed where she had planted, but her shield came back against her hard enough to drive its upper edge into her cheek. The gaunt pushed for another second, all its weight behind the overlapping slabs on its back, then pulled away.

A line of blood ran from Cass's cheek to her jaw.

"Again," she said.

Theo had drawn both swords. "I can't get around her on that bridge."

Pip leaned left, then right, trying to see past Cass. "The plates open under its front legs when it charges. Not much."

"Can you mark the joint?" Cass asked.

"When it lifts an arm."

Juno and Theo lowered the stretcher to the walkway. Doc moved in beside Ilyas, who turned his head towards the bridge and coughed into the blanket strapped across his chest.

"You need to keep moving," he said when he could speak again.

"We know," Doc said. "Breathe first."

The doorkeeper backed to the threshold. It lowered its head and struck the concrete once with its fist.

Cass moved another step towards it.

"Wait," Ren said.

She stopped without looking back.

Ren released Carry. Doc's pack returned to him while he was crouched beside Ilyas. It pulled one shoulder down, and Juno caught the pack before it dragged him across the stretcher.

"Sorry," Ren said.

"Warn me next time, child."

"I can take the upkeep from Hold Fast," Ren told Cass. "You still take the hit. I only take what the skill costs you."

Cass glanced back. "You already spent Attend and Escort."

"I'm not at first symptom yet."

"Yet," Doc said.

The doorkeeper hit the concrete again.

Cass looked at the blood on the inside of her shield, then at the bridge. "One charge. You call the stop."

"I need you to agree to the burden."

"I agree. Take Hold Fast for one charge."

Ren used Carry.

The weight was nothing like Doc's pack. There was no pull on his shoulders and no new strain in his legs. Instead, the upkeep from Cass's skill settled into his attention with the pressure already waiting for it. Holding her in place against the next impact was going to cost her Focus. Now it would cost Ren instead.

Pip moved to the right-hand rail. Juno took a wrapped coal pot from her satchel.

"I can heat the joint when it opens," Juno said. "I don't know how much armour is behind the first plate, so I need Theo ready before I start."

Theo rolled one shoulder and looked at Cass. "Make me some room."

Cass walked towards the doorkeeper.

It charged as soon as she passed the middle of the bridge.

Pip raised her bow. Cass planted and lifted the shield. The doorkeeper's right arm came off the deck for its final stride, exposing a narrow fold of dark hide beneath the slabbed shoulder.

A red mark appeared over the joint.

The collision reached Ren through Carry.

His attention narrowed around the single fact of Cass remaining in place. The skill took everything the doorkeeper put into the shield and billed him for it. He could still see the bridge and hear Juno calling the angle, but both had become things he had to keep picking up after they slipped away.

Juno opened her hand. A thin line of fire crossed beside Cass's shield and struck the marked joint. She held it there just long enough for the dark hide to pull tight.

Theo stepped past Ren.

"Now," Cass said.

She turned the shield sideways and gave him half the bridge.

Theo used [EDGE]. The burst carried his first step to Cass's shoulder. His second took him past the shield as the doorkeeper dragged its weight back. He cut into the heated joint. The sword landed, and [FOLLOW-THROUGH] fed the next cut.

The doorkeeper swung its free arm across the bridge.

Cass moved her shield over Theo and used [SHELTER]. The blow that would have reached him struck her instead. Her body turned with it. Hold Fast kept her feet on the deck, and Carry made the skill cost her nothing, but neither skill softened the impact. She hit the handrail with her left shoulder and stayed upright.

Ren's vision tightened at the edges.

"Tunnel vision," he said. "I'm releasing it."

He let Carry go.

The upkeep returned to Cass while the doorkeeper was still pressing against her shield. Her head jerked once as the Focus cost arrived. She did not move her feet.

Theo's second cut landed beside the first. The chain made his third faster. The doorkeeper dropped its weight towards him, and Cass shoved the shield between them before it could pin him against the rail.

"Back," she said.

Theo stepped behind her. He had opened the joint, but the doorkeeper could still put weight on the arm.

Juno closed her hand. The flame went out. She wrapped the coal while she watched the gaunt retreat to the door.

"I can make the opening bigger," she said. "Give me another pass."

"No," Ren said.

Everyone looked at him.

He had one hand on the bridge rail. His sight had stopped narrowing after he released Carry, but the dark rim had not gone away.

"I called first symptom. I'm done with skills for the day."

Cass watched him for a moment. "Then you're done. Get behind Doc."

The order hurt more than the Focus did. Ren stepped away from the bridge anyway.

Doc moved between Ren and the fight. "Keep telling me if it changes."

"It isn't getting worse."

"That was not the question."

"I will."

Cass rolled her left shoulder once. It moved, although not through its full range.

Theo saw it. "You can't take another one like that."

"I can take one."

"That's what you said about the last one."

"Then make this one useful."

Pip kept the mark on the wounded joint. "The plate above it isn't sitting flat now. If it charges on that arm, the gap will open farther."

Theo looked down the bridge. "I need it to miss Cass."

"It won't," Pip said. "She's closest to the door."

Cass studied the doorkeeper. It had returned to the exact place where they had first seen it. Blood ran between two plates under its right shoulder, but it showed no interest in protecting the wound. It faced Cass and waited for her to approach its threshold again.

"Juno," Cass said. "Can you put heat through the cut without feeding it more?"

Juno looked at the coal in her hand. "Yes. For a short burst. It won't kill it."

"It doesn't need to."

Cass handed Theo her hammer.

He took it and nearly lost the head towards the deck. "You carry this thing through whole Gates?"

"Both hands. Hit the shoulder plate when Pip calls it."

Theo sheathed one sword. He held the hammer across his body and tested the space between the rails.

Cass walked onto the bridge for the third time.

Ren stood behind Doc with nothing running. The urge to designate Theo was immediate. Escort could keep his feet under him when he swung the unfamiliar hammer. It could get him through the gap faster. Ren still had clear hearing. The tunnel around his vision had not closed, but it had not deepened either.

He could help for one more exchange. That did not mean he should.

The doorkeeper charged.

Cass did not plant in the centre this time. She kept moving until the gaunt committed its weight to the wounded forelimb, then stepped behind her shield and angled it towards the left rail.

The doorkeeper struck off-centre. Cass went backwards instead of using Hold Fast. Her boots slid over the deck, and the shield turned the gaunt's head towards the handrail. One slabbed shoulder hit the steel hard enough to bend it out over the water.

"Open," Pip said.

Juno sent the flame through the cut.

The doorkeeper lifted its right arm away from the heat. The loose plate rose with it.

Theo came in beside Cass with the hammer held in both hands. He planted, swung, and hit the underside of the raised plate.

The plate broke away at one end. His hammer stopped against the joint beneath it with a sound Ren felt through the deck.

Theo let the hammer fall and drew his sword. He cut into the opening once, twice, and then the chain took over. The doorkeeper tried to turn inside the width of the bridge. Cass drove the shield into its head and kept it facing the rail. Pip's arrow went into the joint beside Theo's blade.

Juno stopped feeding the coal.

Theo's fourth cut severed something inside the shoulder. The doorkeeper's right side dropped. It struck the damaged handrail, which folded under its weight.

For a moment, half the gaunt hung over the water. Its left hand scraped across the deck and found Cass's ankle.

She brought the edge of the shield down across its wrist. The grip loosened. Theo kicked the broken shoulder plate clear, put one boot against the gaunt's side, and pushed.

The doorkeeper fell into the sluice channel.

It hit the water on its back. The slabs kept it at the surface for several seconds while its left arm reached for the bridge. Then water entered the spaces between the plates, and it sank beside the tower wall.

Theo stayed bent over the rail until the last grey shape disappeared. "Do they drown?"

"That one was full of holes," Pip said.

Cass took the hammer from him. "Get the stretcher."

The concrete doorway stood empty.

The door opened into a square room at the bottom of the sluice tower. Rusted control wheels covered one wall. A concrete stair turned around a central column and climbed out of sight.

Cass and Pip checked the first landing before Theo and Juno carried Ilyas inside. Doc followed the stretcher. Ren came last and shut the steel door behind them, though the latch had rusted away and the door opened again by a hand's width.

The room smelled drier than the warehouse. Water moved through pipes beneath the floor, and the vibration came up through Ren's boots.

Doc made everyone stop on the first landing. He checked Cass's shoulder while she held the shield upright with her other hand.

"Bruised," he said. "Possibly cracked. You can still use it, which is not the same as it being fine."

"Can you Mend it?"

"Nothing is open."

Cass adjusted the shield strap so it sat farther from the joint.

Doc turned to Ren. The dark rim around Ren's sight had begun to recede, but it was still there when he looked towards the corners of the room.

"Better," Ren said before Doc asked. "Not gone. Hearing is normal."

"You stay off your skills until tomorrow."

"I know."

"Say it anyway."

"I'm done until tomorrow."

Doc nodded and went back to Ilyas.

The coughing had eased while they were still, but Ilyas's breathing sounded rougher in the enclosed stairwell. Doc listened against his back and told him they would stop again at the top.

"How far?" Ilyas asked.

Pip looked up the central shaft. "Five landings to the upper gantry. About twenty metres."

"Then don't stop halfway for me."

"We'll stop when Doc says," Juno said. "You can be difficult after we get outside."

Theo took the front of the stretcher this time, walking backwards up the stairs while Juno lifted from below. Pip went ahead and checked every landing. Cass followed beside Juno where the stair was wide enough. Doc handed Ren his pack before they started up. Without Carry, its full weight dragged at the straps and made him lean forward on every step.

Nobody offered to take it from him. It was ordinary weight, and carrying it gave him something to do.

The second landing had a window looking over the warehouse. From higher up, Ren could see the route they had taken through the racking as a broken line of green marks. The receiving platform was hidden behind a wall of containers. The underwater camera's feed could still be running somewhere below, filming whatever moved through the junction after them.

He turned away from the window and kept climbing.

At the fourth landing, Ilyas began coughing again. Theo and Juno set the stretcher down across the stairs. Doc knelt beside him until the fit passed. Cass did not ask how much time it cost.

Pip checked the stopwatch when they moved again and kept the number to herself.

The final stair ended at a steel door. Pip opened it a few centimetres, looked through the gap, then pushed it wide.

The upper gantry ran along the warehouse wall at the level of the highest racking. It was broad enough for the stretcher and built from heavier steel than the maintenance walkways below. For the first thirty metres, it looked intact.

Then it ended in open air.

A section longer than the sluice bridge had torn away from the wall and fallen across the racking. The far end of the return route remained attached near the receiving platform, but there was no steel between the two halves. The gap was too wide for their rope.

Pip took out the wet map and flattened it against the door. She checked the printed gantry line, then looked across the warehouse again.

"The return route is gone," she said.

Ilyas closed his eyes.

Cass stepped onto the gantry and looked in both directions. To the left, the broken route pointed back towards the aperture. To the right, the steel continued along the wall, crossed above three flooded aisles, and joined the crane structure near the middle of the warehouse.

Ren had seen that route on the plan. The crane cab held the Core. It also had a maintenance catwalk running from its far side towards the receiving platform.

"Is that catwalk still connected?" he asked.

Pip raised her binoculars. She took her time with the answer.

"From here to the crane cab, yes. I can't see the section on the other side."

Cass looked at Ilyas on the stretcher, then at the route leading deeper into the warehouse.

"We check it," she said. "Extraction is still the job."

Pip folded the map. Theo and Juno lifted the stretcher, and the team turned towards the crane cab.
