# Human-prose blind evaluation rubric

## Purpose

Compare the frozen current system against the candidate three-skill system without rewarding mere compliance, novelty, or visible effort. The target is story prose that feels written by a genuine person and makes Michael want to continue reading.

## Blind procedure

- Present each pair as `A` and `B`; do not reveal system labels.
- Randomise independently per scenario.
- Read prose only. Do not expose generation packets, process notes, or audit reports.
- Judge the first reading before inspecting mechanical checks.
- A tie is valid. Do not force a winner when differences are cosmetic.
- Mechanical defects can overturn a preference when they break literal action, fair mechanics, or a named high-confidence reader rule.

## Primary dimensions

Score each from 1–5.

### 1. Genuine human texture

- **1:** recognisably model-shaped; uniform polish, explanatory closure, repeated rhetorical armature, or checklist-steered phrasing.
- **3:** readable and mostly unobtrusive, but with recurring engineered texture.
- **5:** local choices feel caused by a particular mind, situation, and rhythm rather than by writing guidance.

### 2. Desire to continue

- **1:** scene completes the assignment without creating curiosity or emotional investment.
- **3:** competent momentum with some interest in the result.
- **5:** the reader actively wants the next beat because the situation, relationship, or mechanism remains alive.

### 3. Character specificity

- **1:** characters are functional role-holders or share one polished voice.
- **3:** motives are clear and some behaviour is individual.
- **5:** attention, misreading, tactics, speech, and practical choices could belong only to these characters.

### 4. Clarity without visible management

- **1:** confusing, over-explained, or visibly engineered to avoid confusion.
- **3:** clear but occasionally instructional or managed.
- **5:** action, stakes, and world information are effortless on first read.

### 5. Rhythm and narrative weight

- **1:** repetitive cadence, fragment chains, buttoned paragraphs, or uniform sentence polish.
- **3:** serviceable variation with some patterned passages.
- **5:** sentence and paragraph weight changes naturally with attention, pressure, action, and aftermath.

## Scenario-specific checks

### Social pressure

- Dialogue performs social actions rather than exchanging polished positions.
- Nera changes tactics because of a specific observation.
- Leverage changes without a neat moral or contractual resolution.

### Causal action

- The teleport rule remains legible through action.
- The solution is fair, causal, and choice-driven.
- Tactical explanation serves the rescue rather than becoming bookkeeping.

### Quiet aftermath

- Mixed emotion is legible through work, avoidance, and mistaken assumptions.
- The mentor has an agenda and can be wrong.
- The last ordinary action carries consequence without summarising a lesson.

## Regression checks

Flag exact evidence for:

- unclear literal action;
- first-use world terms left ungrounded;
- polished thesis dialogue;
- generic emotion or stock body reactions;
- pithy balanced clauses shared across speakers;
- explanatory or moral closure;
- repeated structural devices close together;
- arithmetic or logistics detail that does not change stakes; and
- violations of the task's stated mechanics or causal requirements.

## Decision rule

A candidate passes the evaluation stage when:

1. it wins at least two of the three pairwise scenario judgements;
2. its mean score is higher across the five primary dimensions;
3. it does not introduce a critical clarity, causality, mechanics, or reader-rule regression; and
4. no win depends solely on more ornate or more aggressively edited prose.

Michael's blind preference is the adoption authority. Model graders provide diagnostic evidence, not final taste.
